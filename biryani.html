Vegetable Biryani Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup Basmati rice
  • 2 cups mixed vegetables (e.g., carrots, peas, beans)
  • 1 large onion, thinly sliced
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1/4 cup yogurt
  • 2 tablespoons biryani masala
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 2-3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1-inch piece of ginger, minced
  • 2 green chilies, slit
  • 2 tablespoons cooking oil or ghee
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh cilantro and mint leaves for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Wash the Basmati rice under running water until the water runs clear. Soak the rice in water for 30 minutes, then drain and set aside.
  2. Heat the cooking oil or ghee in a large heavy-bottomed pan. Add the cumin seeds and let them sizzle for a few seconds.
  3. Add the sliced onions and sauté them until they turn golden brown.
  4. Add the minced garlic, ginger, and green chilies. Sauté for another couple of minutes until the raw smell disappears.
  5. Add the chopped tomatoes and cook until they become soft and the oil starts to separate from the mixture.
  6. Stir in the biryani masala and cook for a minute to release its flavors.
  7. Add the mixed vegetables and cook for 2-3 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  8. Now, add the drained Basmati rice and gently stir to mix everything together. Be careful not to break the rice grains.
  9. Pour in the yogurt and mix well. Add salt to taste.
  10. Pour 2 cups of water over the rice and vegetables. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to low, cover the pan with a tight-fitting lid, and simmer for 15-20 minutes, or until the rice is cooked and the vegetables are tender.
  11. Once cooked, turn off the heat and let the biryani sit, covered, for 5-10 minutes to allow the flavors to meld.
  12. Garnish with fresh cilantro and mint leaves before serving.
  13. Serve the vegetable biryani hot with raita or a side salad.
