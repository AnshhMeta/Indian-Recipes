Dosa Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup rice
  • 1/4 cup urad dal (split black gram)
  • 1/4 teaspoon fenugreek seeds (optional)
  • 1/4 cup poha (flattened rice)
  • Water for soaking
  • Salt to taste
  • Oil or ghee for cooking

Instructions:

  1. Wash the rice, urad dal, and fenugreek seeds (if using) together under running water until the water runs clear. Place them in a bowl and cover with enough water. Allow them to soak for 4-6 hours or overnight.
  2. Wash the poha under running water and drain. Add the poha to the soaked rice and dal mixture.
  3. Drain the soaked mixture and transfer it to a blender or wet grinder. Grind into a smooth batter, adding water as needed. The batter should have a pouring consistency, similar to pancake batter.
  4. Transfer the batter to a large bowl. Add salt to taste and mix well. Allow the batter to ferment in a warm place for 8-12 hours or until it doubles in volume. Fermentation time may vary depending on the temperature.
  5. After fermentation, the batter will be bubbly and have a slight sour smell. Mix it gently to combine the batter once again.
  6. Heat a non-stick dosa pan or griddle over medium-high heat. Once hot, reduce the heat to medium-low.
  7. Grease the pan lightly with oil or ghee. Take a ladleful of dosa batter and pour it onto the center of the pan.
  8. Spread the batter in a circular motion from the center outwards to make a thin, even dosa. You can make it as thin or thick as you prefer.
  9. Drizzle a little oil or ghee around the edges of the dosa and cook until the bottom turns golden brown and crispy.
  10. Flip the dosa and cook the other side until it's cooked through and lightly browned.
  11. Remove the dosa from the pan and serve hot with your favorite chutney or sambar.
